Background

Fabrication, Computation &
Translational Science

I'm a Biomedical/Mechanical Engineer at KLS Martin Group, where I develop simulation-based validation workflows using ANSYS HFSS and Mechanical, led two FDA 510(k) clearances end-to-end, and served as the technical SME in an FDA Pre-Submission teleconference. My work bridges computation, fabrication, and regulatory science.

At the Arany Lab (SUNY Buffalo), I built a patient-specific biological construct workflow that directly mirrors what Dr. Sah's CTE Lab does at the tissue engineering scale: medical imaging → 3D anatomical modeling → mesh optimization → SLA/DLP 3D printing → ICP-based anatomical fit validation (n=34, p>0.05). I also evaluated mechanical properties of 3D-printed vs. traditionally fabricated constructs using FEA — a methodology central to the CTE Lab's quantitative characterization approach.

Dr. Sah's research goal is to pave the way for successful tissue-engineered total joint replacement by regenerating cartilage that fills defects with load-bearing tissue and integrates with surrounding host tissue — combining quantitative experiments across multiple biological scales with systematic model-based analyses of biomechanics and biotransport. My multi-scale fabrication and computational modeling background is built exactly for this.
